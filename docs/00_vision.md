# Haven Product Vision & Mission

## 1. Vision Statement
**Haven** is a community-first knowledge network designed to connect people without the noise of engagement-bait algorithms. We believe the internet should be built around cooperative communities rather than individuals chasing virality.

## 2. Mission
To build the operating system for online communities, enabling them to easily discover, self-organize, collaborate, and retain their collective knowledge permanently.

## 3. The Problems with Today's Platforms
* **Twitter/X (Centralized Broadcast)**: Promotes individual outrage and algorithmic feeds optimized for screen-time/ads rather than genuine connection.
* **Discord (Ephemeral Chat)**: Great for real-time talk, but information gets lost rapidly in infinite scroll, leading to repeated questions and fragmented knowledge.
* **Reddit (Anonymous Aggregator)**: Driven by karma farming, anonymous trolling, and lack of cohesive organizational tools for long-term project collaboration.
* **Facebook Groups (Legacy Social)**: Outdated user experience, heavy advertising, and data privacy concerns.
* **Mastodon (Decentralized Microblogging)**: Highly technical onboarding, server fragmentation makes discovery difficult for average users, and lack of built-in tooling for wikis or projects.

## 4. Core Philosophy: Communities as First-Class Citizens
In Haven, all interactions start and end within **Communities (Servers)**. 
* There are no global user-to-user follower timelines that act as the default landing page.
* Feeds are strictly derived from the communities a user has joined.
* Reputation is community-specific and earned through contributions, helpfulness, and verified expertise, rather than passive followings.

## 5. The Ultimate Differentiator: Institutional Memory
Unlike existing social applications where posts disappear down a timeline after 48 hours, Haven focuses on **knowledge preservation**. Every major discussion, answered question, roadmap, or resource list is captured and synthesized by AI, vetted by the community, and integrated into a growing, structured Knowledge Graph. As a community ages, its value increases as it builds a searchable, permanent brain.
